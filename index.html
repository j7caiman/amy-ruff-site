About

Amy Ruff lives next to her beloved alma mater, Duke University, & enjoys in-person as well as online teaching, tutoring, & test prepping with students from Durham-Chapel Hill to Raleigh to Holly Springs-Fuquay to Hillsborough-Mebane in her areas of specialty: literature, Spanish, psychology, philosophy, SAT/ACT/AP test prep, & study skills (Dartmouth’s SQ3R; Adler’s How to Read a Book; & Gardner’s Theory of Multiple Intelligences).

Having completed her BA at Duke with double majors in English & religion, Amy also earned a Certificate in Duke’s rigorous interdisciplinary Women’s Studies Program. While an undergraduate & after graduation, Amy worked in editing for the Duke University Press & has continued to freelance edit. Amy & her husband have been members of Grace Church in Chapel Hill since they were Duke undergrads.

Having homeschooled her own children from birth through high-school graduation, Amy continues to support the homeschooling community by offering group-class tutorials through which she imparts her contagious passion for learning & shares her seasoned skills for teaching. Remarkably capable at identifying & developing the potential in her students, Amy's energetic & intuitive style enables students to grow in their knowledge of subject matter.

Amy also cultivates intrapersonal intelligence in her students, leading them to self-understanding. By equipping students to align self-knowledge with their envisioned futures, Amy engenders confidence in her pupils for making informed choices during the process of seeking well-matched placement into collegiate-academic & trade-development settings: from Harvard, Duke, & Stanford to UNC, NCSU, & Appalachian to community colleges across NC, Amy's students continually thrive on diverse academic, trade, & professional paths.

Of Argentine-Italian descent & hailing from Italian roots in Pittsburgh, PA, Sra. Amy's style of teaching Spanish is full of tango! Having studied Spanish in boarding school & been awarded a scholarship from the Spanish National Honor Society, Amy continued with advanced Spanish studies at Duke & served on short-term missions as a Spanish interpreter. From the rudiments of grammar & vocabulary to immersion in cooking & dancing, Sra. Amy’s students learn to love Latina language & culture!

Always eager to serve & support the local & global community, Amy is a member of Durham’s Centro Hispano, a volunteer with the YMCA, a promoter of OAR (Organizing against Racism), a contributor to Grace Church’s C3 (Cross-Culture on Campus), & a supporter of two overseas NGOs.
