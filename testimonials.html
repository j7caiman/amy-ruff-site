Kendrick V., Harvard 2017 (Majors in Economics and Computer Science): "Mrs. Ruff’s English classes prepared me exceptionally well for the writing demands of Harvard. I thrived in my required freshman writing course, earning an A. Specifically, I found my ability to structure a paper to far exceed that of my peers. Now, as a senior, I am known among my peers as an excellent editor and have edited several senior theses for friends this spring. However, to say Mrs. Ruff’s courses improved only my writing ability would be insufficient. The benefits of her courses have extended beyond English classes. All courses at Harvard require the ability to think clearly and formulate a sound argument. I attribute my success at Harvard, in large part, to Mrs. Ruff’s development of my critical-thinking skills."

Joan M., a Chapel Hill mom & professional teacher: My son, Marlow, took Amy Ruff's Spanish class during his 7th and 8th grade years. Amy's Spanish 1 and 2 use an older textbook because, she says, "The newer text books just don’t cover enough material—especially for their price."  Amy and Marlow decided to play it safe and put Marlow in Spanish 2 this year, his freshman year at Chapel Hill High School. Well, he should really have been placed in Spanish 3 as he has been top of his CHHS Spanish class grade-wise all year with absolutely no effort on his part. In other words, what Amy Ruff calls Spanish 1 and 2 is really high school Spanish 1, 2, and at least a 1/2 year of Spanish 3. She covers a lot of material in that one to one & a quarter hour once a week! Marlow just received the results from the National Spanish 2 Exam and placed third in the State of North Carolina. Considering he has learned no new material in Spanish 2 at CHHS, this is Marlow and Amy Ruff's win! She's that good. Do I recommend Amy Ruff? Yes! Two thumbs up!
