During a two-hour intensive with a textbook in hand, students learn how to outline a chapter, unit, or module. Dartmouth’s SQ3R Study Method combines visual, auditory, & kinesthetic-learning styles through the following steps: quickly SURVEYING material to gain an overview; creating QUESTIONS (not statements) that form “bins into which info is placed” or “hooks upon which info hangs” within the outline framework; READING selectively by employing Mortimer Adler’s Inspectional Reading techniques of Systematic Skimming; RECITING info to engage auditory processing; & REVIEWING info to reinforce understanding of & knowledge about the material at hand.
